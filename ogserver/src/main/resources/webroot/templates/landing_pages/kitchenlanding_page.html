Types of Kitchen

L-shaped kitchen

The L-shaped kitchen is among the most versatile of modular kitchen layouts and is popular as well. It works well for most homes, irrespective of the size of the space you have for your kitchen. It creates an efficient cooking space that makes room for appliances, both large and small, as well as spacious cabinetry. Installed along right angles of walls in a kitchen, it leaves an open area in the centre that you can use to bring in a cooking island or even a small dining table and chairs.

U-shaped kitchen

Ideal for the medium-sized home, the U-shaped kitchen layout is perfect for efficient cooking when you manage the kitchen on your own. This layout is spread out across three walls, with two parallel to each other to bring about the U-shape. The layout is characterized by the fact that no traffic lanes pass through the entire work area. The compact size ensures that the perfect kitchen triangle is achieved and you have a cooking space that you will love to spend time in.

Straight kitchen

For the small home that aspires to look trendy and completely functional at the same time, the straight kitchen is the perfect solution. It provides you storage as well as cooking, prepping and cleaning areas, all the while taking up minimal space across one wall. This widens the working area of your kitchen, allowing you to do more. This is the simplest of kitchen layouts and can be easily installed, allowing you easy access to everything you need.

Parallel kitchen

Also known as the galley kitchen, this layout creates a walkway between two functional counters placed opposite each other. Each of the counters have dedicated space for appliances, washing, prepping and cooking. These are ideal for kitchens that are narrow or if you want to have an extended space for a rectangular kitchen. No matter what the size of the house, this kitchen layout works well.
